Leeds Castle Website

Leeds Castle has over 900 years of history and has been used as a Norman stronghold, the private property of six of England's medieval Queens, a Palace used by Henry VIII's first wife Catherine of Aragon, an elegant early 20th century playground for the rich and famous, as Lady Baille, the last private owner, entertained high society down from London for the weekends and now our wedding!

Directions

Leeds Castle Directions

Arriving by Car

Please enter the estate via the Main Gate. Please drive straight through the main car park until you reach the Ticket Box on your right hand side. A member of staff will be at the barrier in front of you, and they will direct you to the castle car park.

Arriving by Taxi

Please enter via the Main Gate, ask the taxi driver to drive straight up to the barrier where a steward will direct you to a drop off area. When being collected at the end of the day please could you be collected through the Broomfield Gate, the taxi will collect you just off castle Island. We would encourage you to pre-book taxi’s.

Staying at the Stable Courtyard

Please enter via the Broomfield Gate not the Main Gate. Press the intercom button to Security and then drive to the guest car park, please check-in at the Reception.

Staying at the Maiden's Tower

Please follow the arriving by car instructions. There is transport available for those that are staying overnight in the Maiden’s Tower to assist with luggage and for any day guests with walking difficulties. For those that do not have walking difficultie please take the short walk to the Maiden’s Tower.
